# FORMAL FREEDOM OF INFORMATION ACT REQUEST TEMPLATES

**Legal Basis:** Freedom of Information Act 2011 (Nigeria)  
**Response Deadline:** 7 working days from receipt

## TEMPLATE 1: LOAN AGREEMENT REQUEST (DMO)

**TO:** Debt Management Office Nigeria  
**EMAIL:** info@dmo.gov.ng  
**SUBJECT:** Freedom of Information Request - [Specific Loan] Agreement

Dear Director-General,

Pursuant to Section 1 of the Freedom of Information Act 2011, I hereby request access to the following public documents:

**REQUESTED DOCUMENTS:**

1. Complete loan agreement for [LOAN_ID from database - e.g., "Lagos-Ibadan Railway, China Exim Bank, 2017"]
2. All amendments or supplementary agreements to said loan
3. Disbursement schedule showing:
   - Date of each disbursement
   - Amount disbursed
   - Recipient entity/ministry
   - Purpose/project milestone achieved
4. Repayment schedule showing:
   - Principal repayment dates and amounts
   - Interest payment dates and amounts
   - Current outstanding balance
5. List of all contractors paid from loan proceeds, including:
   - Contractor name
   - Contract value
   - Services/goods provided
   - Payment dates and amounts

**LEGAL BASIS:**
- FOIA 2011, Section 1: Right of access to public records
- Section 2(3): Public institutions must ensure records are properly maintained
- This information concerns public funds and is not subject to exemption under Sections 11-14

**DEADLINE:** Under Section 4(a) FOIA 2011, you are required to respond within 7 working days of receipt.

**IF DENIED:** Under Section 7, I reserve the right to apply to the Court for an order compelling disclosure.

**APPLICANT DETAILS:**
- Full Name: [Your Name]
- Address: [Your Address]
- Email: [Your Email]
- Phone: [Your Phone]
- Date: [Date]

I request response via email to expedite delivery.

Respectfully,  
[Your Signature]

1. **Choose appropriate template** for your specific request

4. **Send via email AND registered mail** (create paper trail)
5. **Keep copies** of everything
6. **Mark calendar** for 7-day deadline
7. **Share publicly** that you sent it (Twitter, Nairaland, etc.)
8. **Follow up systematically** if no response
9. **Escalate to SERAP** if denied without valid exemption
10. **Coordinate with others** - mass FOIA requests are harder to ignore

## VALID EXEMPTIONS (That May Be Claimed)

**FOIA 2011 allows withholding only if:**

- Section 11: International affairs/defense (rarely applies to domestic loans)
- Section 12: Law enforcement (must show specific ongoing investigation)
- Section 13: Personal information (doesn't cover corporate contractors)
- Section 14: Commercial confidentiality (burden on agency to prove)

**MOST REQUESTS ABOVE HAVE NO VALID EXEMPTION**

✓ You CANNOT be arrested for filing FOIA requests

✓ Agencies MUST respond in 7 days (extension possible with notice)  
✓ You CAN sue if denied  
✓ Courts CAN order disclosure  
✓ Agencies MAY be liable for legal costs if you win
